Turn in off Jones Street at the Stirling Adriatic Centre / Stirling Bowling Club sign.

Follow the driveway in and continue through the car park — the cricket nets are at the rear of the complex, on the right, beside the bowling green.

Parking: park anywhere in the main car park. Please keep the side laneway by the bins clear — it's marked NO PARKING.

3. Getting in

From the car park, walk down the laneway between the two buildings (past the bins) to the cricket entrance — the unit with the white roller door.

  1. Arrive around your booked start time. There's no staff on site — entry is by your personal door code.
  2. Your door code is in your booking confirmation email and push notification (and on the confirmation screen when you book).
  3. On the brick beside the door there are two units. Use the silver Sebury keypad on the right — not the black intercom on the left.
  4. Enter your door code, then press # to open the door.
  5. The roller door opens. Once the entrance is clear it closes automatically on a 30-second timer — nothing to shut.
Your code only works for your session window, so save your confirmation and arrive on time. If it won't open, check you're using it within your booked time and try again — still stuck? Call Julian on 0451 016 151 (any hour).

4. Your lane / bay

There are 5 bays — bowling-machine lanes and run-up nets. The lanes are numbered inside the building, and your booking email tells you which one you're in — walk straight to that number.

Lighting is automatic — your bay's lights come on around your session start, so there are no switches to find.

5. Using the equipment

Bowling machine: step-by-step operating instructions are displayed on the machine itself — follow those. Operate it only as instructed, don't force or modify settings, and always turn it off before collecting balls (see the house rules).

6. House rules

Access & bookings

  • Your door code is active only for your booking. Only people on your booking enter with you.
  • Arrive and finish within your booked time — the next session may be right behind you.
  • No staff are on site — you're responsible for the lane during your booking.

Footwear & surface

  • Clean, flat-soled, non-marking shoes only. No spikes or metal cleats in the building.
  • No food or chewing gum to be consumed in the nets.

Safety

  • Train at your own risk. Appropriate protective gear (helmet, pads, gloves, box) is compulsory.
  • One batter in the lane at a time; everyone else stays outside the net.
  • Only one person operating the machine at a time.
  • Never stand in front of or reach into the bowling machine while it's on. Turn it off before collecting balls.
  • Anyone under 18 must be supervised by an adult at all times.
  • Maximum 3 people per lane.

Equipment & care

  • Operate machines only as instructed — don't force or modify settings.
  • Collect all balls and leave the ball crate on the machine stand at the end of your booking.
  • Do NOT mix crates of balls — anyone found mixing crates will be banned.
  • Report any fault or damage via the app — don't keep using broken gear.
  • Don't hang or climb on the nets.
Urgent faults — any hour:
Call Julian: 0451 016 151

Conduct & neighbours

  • We share the site with a bowling club and back onto homes — keep noise down, especially early morning and evening.
  • No abusive, unsafe or disruptive behaviour.
  • No alcohol, smoking or vaping anywhere on site.

Breach

Misuse, unsafe behaviour or not following these house rules will lead to restriction from future use of the facility.

7. Leaving

  • Take all your belongings and rubbish with you, and bin it.
  • Leave the ball crate on the machine stand (see the house rules).
  • The lights switch off automatically, and the roller door closes on its 30-second timer once the entrance is clear — nothing to lock up.

8. Help & emergencies

Can't get in, or an urgent problem on site? (any hour)
Call Julian: 0451 016 151

Equipment fault or something not working? Report it through the app (My Bookings → your session → Report a problem) so we can fix it and block the lane if needed.

In an emergency, the exits are on the West wall near the run-up nets and the main entrance door. In a fire or serious incident, get out and call 000.
